

Fact Check 1:
Claim: Japan banned COVID-19 vaccines.
Verdict: False.
Explanation: Japan has not banned COVID vaccines. The government and health authorities continue to recommend vaccination. Viral posts claiming a ban are misinformation.

Fact Check 2:
Claim: NASA confirmed the discovery of aliens in 2024.
Verdict: False.
Explanation: NASA has not announced the discovery of extraterrestrial life. Claims circulating online were based on misinterpreted scientific reports.

Fact Check 3:
Claim: Drinking bleach can cure COVID-19.
Verdict: False.
Explanation: Health organizations including the World Health Organization and CDC warn that drinking bleach is dangerous and has no medical benefit.

Fact Check 4:
Claim: The Earth is flat.
Verdict: False.
Explanation: Scientific evidence from satellite imagery, gravity measurements, and space exploration confirms that Earth is spherical.

Fact Check 5:
Claim: 5G networks spread COVID-19.
Verdict: False.
Explanation: Viruses cannot travel through radio waves or mobile networks. This claim has been debunked by scientists and health organizations.

Fact Check 6:
Claim: Vaccines cause autism.
Verdict: False.
Explanation: Numerous scientific studies show no link between vaccines and autism. The original claim was based on a fraudulent study that was later retracted.

Fact Check 7:
Claim: Humans use only 10% of their brain.
Verdict: False.
Explanation: Brain imaging studies show that most regions of the brain have known functions and are active at different times.

Fact Check 8:
Claim: Climate change is a hoax created by scientists.
Verdict: False.
Explanation: Climate change is supported by overwhelming scientific evidence including temperature records, glacier melting, and atmospheric measurements.

Fact Check 9:
Claim: The Great Wall of China is visible from the Moon with the naked eye.
Verdict: False.
Explanation: Astronauts confirm that the Great Wall is generally not visible from the Moon without special equipment.

Fact Check 10:
Claim: Sharks attack humans frequently.
Verdict: Misleading.
Explanation: Shark attacks are extremely rare. Humans are far more dangerous to sharks than sharks are to humans.
